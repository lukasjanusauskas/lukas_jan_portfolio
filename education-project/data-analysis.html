Important note about education attainment

Before we move on there is a definition, that will be very important to this analysis. Education attainment level for a particular education level (for example upper secondary education) means the portion of residents, whose highest level of education is said education level (in our case upper secondary education).

For example a respondent, who has graduated with a bachelors degree will be added to the teritary education attainment level, not to secondary or priamry education attainment levels.

Outliers

To take in consideration the limits of this analysis, I will detect outliers only under one dimension slice (it is possible to detect outliers for each possible dimension configuration or a bigger subset of them). The most important slice, in my opinion, is: sex=Total, Educational level code = ED34 (secondary education) and age=Y15-64 slice.

For outlier detection I will use simple quartile method.

Outliers are identified using limits calculated from the quartiles. Any data point falling below Q1 - 1.5IQR or above Q3 + 1.5IQR is considered an outlier. Some applications also define extreme outliers using a 3.0IQR multiplier instead of 1.5IQR for more stringent detection.

def get_limits(values):
    mask = ~np.isnan(values)
    values_clean = values[mask]
    
    q3, q1 = np.quantile(values_clean, [0.75, 0.25])
    iqr = q3 - q1

    return q3 + 1.5 * iqr, q1 - 1.5 * iqr

Further inquiry

On this outlier list we can see nordic countries - that is not something unexpected. However, what is unexpected is that Malta is part of the list.

There are two main factors that are responsible for this outlier:

  1. Malta is a small island, that does not have much resources, so it relies on human capital.
  2. There are a lot of foreign students and around 2013 they had a surge of foreign students.
  3. Malta was in recovery mode after austerity caused by the 2008 crisis.

Thus Maltese policy makers decided to increase spending in education.

  1. Low female labor fource participation. Only about 30% of women are participating in the labour force.. This may be caused by the high
  2. Low social spending. Turkey does not spend a lot on social programmes (considering their GDP) and thus exacerbating the problem of low activity rates.

Summary

There are many ways one can approach to disect European countries' education-economic profile. In this analysis, focusing on , I form the following main conclusions:

  • A key insight is that tertiary education levels have a stronger relationship with activity rates than secondary or primary education levels.
  • Teacher shortages exacerbated by low wages can cause high pupil to teacher ratios, thus possibly lowering educational standards of the country.
  • Small spending in social programmes does seem to be detrimental to the activity rates of a country.
